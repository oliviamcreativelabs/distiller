How Whiskey is Made

To make whiskey, you need three ingredients: grain, hot water, and yeast. The water breaks down the grain and extracts the sugar from it. This is known as the MASH and is done before fermentation. The yeast then feeds on the sugar to create alcohol. At this point the liquid is technically a beer (sometimes called the “wash”), and not potable. At this point distillation can begin.
